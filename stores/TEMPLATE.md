# store-name.md

<https://store-name.md> — what they sell; physical presence if any.

## Stock detection

How to reliably tell whether a product is in stock — the exact page element
or API call, verified against ground truth (an actual add-to-cart or a known
in/out-of-stock pair). List known traps: hidden "notify me" modals,
commented-out markup, elements toggled by JS.

## Prices

Where the price lives in the page. Pricing reputation: fair/overpriced
overall, or per category. Retail vs. business prices, discounts, price
matching.

## Shipping

Delivery options, costs, restrictions (regions, weight, COD availability).

## Payment

Accepted methods and restrictions (card online, cash on delivery, transfer
for legal entities only, …).

## Scraping notes

URL patterns, selectors, embedded JSON, required User-Agent/cookies, rate
limits. If a scraper directory exists, point to it instead of duplicating.

## History

Dated notes: purchases made, support experiences, incidents.
